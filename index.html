Introduction

Around the world, football (or soccer in North America), is played by more than 240 million people, a statistic generated by the Federation Internationale de Football Association (FIFA). The number of fans around the world can be doubled, with the 2022 World Cup opening match and finals having a worldwide total of 550 million viewers and 1.5 billion viewers respectively. SOURCE

Although organized association football only became a thing in the 19th century, the origins of the game can be seen more than 2000 years ago, in ancient civilizations such as China, Greece, and Rome, where the balls were made of rock or stuffed animal hides. There's even evidence that shows similar games being played in older Mesoamerican cultures.

The Football Association (FA) was created in 1863, in England, and an initial ruleset and dimensions of the matchball were standardized, although these rules are still changing to this day. The biggest change was banning carrying the ball with your hands, separating association football and rugby...

By the 20th century, football had spread all across Europe rather than just the British territories, leading to more conflict with international play. In 1904, FA representatives from Belgium, Denmark, France, the Netherlands, Spain, Sweden, and Switzerland formed the Fédération Internationale de Football Association (FIFA).
